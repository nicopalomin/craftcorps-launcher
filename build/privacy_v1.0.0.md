## 3. Information We Collect

### 3.1 Account & Identifier Data

We generate or process internal identifiers, including:

* an internal user ID,
* optional account credentials (username, email) if provided,
* account status and settings.

An account may exist without a username or email address unless required for a specific feature.

Usernames, when provided, may be linked to internal identifiers to enable account-based features.

### 3.2 Device Identifiers & Session Data

To operate the service, maintain sessions, and prevent abuse, we may process:

* a launcher-generated device or instance identifier,
* session identifiers,
* session start and end times,
* app version information.

These identifiers are used to maintain continuity, security, and abuse prevention. They are **not** used to access personal files or track users across unrelated applications.

Depending on jurisdiction, device identifiers may be considered personal data.

### 3.3 IP Addresses

IP addresses may be processed **only when required**, such as for:

* security and abuse prevention,
* rate limiting,
* crash diagnostics.

IP addresses are not used for advertising or long-term tracking and may be truncated, anonymized, or retained only for short periods where possible.

### 3.4 Authentication & Minecraft Accounts

When you authenticate using third-party providers (such as the **Mojang / Microsoft API**):

* authentication is handled by the provider,
* CraftCorps receives confirmation necessary to launch or connect the game.

Minecraft authentication tokens and credentials are stored **locally on your device** and are **not stored on CraftCorps servers**.

In the future, we may offer optional features that involve server-side token handling. If this occurs, this Privacy Policy will be updated and users will be informed.

### 3.5 No-Account & Pre-Login Usage

Before account creation or login, we may process limited data such as:

* anonymous or provisional identifiers,
* session-level activity,
* basic technical signals.

This data is used only to operate the launcher, enforce safeguards, and enable a smooth onboarding experience. It is not used to build long-term user profiles.

### 3.6 Analytics & Usage Events

We collect usage and interaction events to understand how the launcher and features are used. These may include:

* feature usage events,
* navigation or interaction events,
* timestamps,
* associated internal user or session identifiers (where available).

Some analytics data may be linked to an account after login. Other analytics may remain **anonymous or pseudonymous**.

Analytics data may be considered personal data depending on how it can be linked to an individual.

### 3.7 Analytics Profiles

We may maintain analytics profiles associated with internal identifiers to support product features and insights, including:

* first launch or login timestamps,
* last activity time,
* theme or preference settings,
* country or region (derived, when available),
* playtime metrics,
* streaks or participation counters.

These profiles are used to improve user experience and enable feature-related functionality.

### 3.8 Hardware & System Information

We may collect limited hardware and system information such as:

* operating system and version,
* CPU, GPU, and RAM details.

This data is used for:

* compatibility checks,
* diagnostics,
* performance analysis,
* abuse prevention.

We do not access personal files or unrelated device contents.

### 3.9 Playtime & Activity Metrics

We track playtime and related activity signals to:

* display playtime information to you,
* enable beta, cosmetic, or promotional features,
* determine eligibility for discretionary rewards,
* detect abuse or automated behavior.

Playtime tracking does not include monitoring in-game chat, content, or communications.

### 3.10 Server Interaction & Statistics

We process server-related interaction data, including:

* views, clicks, joins, and votes,
* join outcomes,
* aggregated and daily statistics.

Much of this data is aggregated at the **server level** rather than the individual user level, but may temporarily involve user or session identifiers for integrity and abuse prevention.

### 3.11 Abuse & Security Signals

We process limited technical and interaction signals to protect the platform, including:

* repeated or abnormal interaction patterns,
* vote or engagement manipulation indicators,
* automated or scripted behavior signals.

These signals may be evaluated using automated, weighted systems and are used solely for security and integrity purposes.

### 3.12 Crash & Diagnostic Data

If the launcher crashes or encounters errors, we may collect:

* crash reports,
* app version and platform,
* process type,
* optional IP address,
* optional internal user or session identifier.

Crash data is used only for debugging and stability improvements.

## 4. What We Do **Not** Collect

* sell personal data,
* use data for advertising networks,
* monitor in-game chat or content,
* access personal files,
* intentionally track users across unrelated applications or services.
